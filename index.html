Introducing ReelBooster

A revolutionary startup giving creatives and audiences the power to shape the future of the entertainment industry

Amidst the Hollywood protests, where writers from WGA and actors from SAG-AFTRA are fighting for equitable compensation, especially in the uncertainty and disruption brought about by new advances in AI, the entertainment industry stands at a critical juncture. The rise of streaming services has resulted in reduced compensation for creatives, who previously relied on residuals from box-office earnings and the protection of their rights, including ownership of their likenesses. All of these income streams are now at risk. Furthermore, the studio system has become risk averse, resorting to recycling existing IPs and generating lacklustre content, which has left audiences underwhelmed and sent revenues plummeting. Now more than ever people crave fresh and original experiences, which the studios are largely failing to deliver.

To address this, a groundbreaking startup has emerged to challenge the status quo and inject new life into the film industry. ReelBooster is a revolutionary platform on a mission to empower talented filmmakers who would normally be ignored by large studios and streaming platforms, to share their stories. ReelBooster aims to achieve this by offering innovative funding models and fostering stronger connections with targeted audiences. In turn, audiences can take a more proactive role in bringing their desired films to life.

ReelBooster harnesses recent breakthroughs in the tech space, particularly AI and Web3 (blockchain with decentralised ownership), reducing the need to rely on traditional industry gatekeepers whose profit-driven motives may not prioritise the interests of creatives and audiences.

Reelbooster was founded by Carl Peterken, a seasoned tech industry veteran and filmmaker with over two decades of experience building engaging solutions for many organisations across the globe. Hailing from New Zealand, he now spends his time between London & Los Angeles. Carl has been involved in many innovative ventures, including the AI/web3 metaverse company Futureverse (through Altered State Machine, which includes partnerships with Fifa & Muhammad Ali). He also led the front-end engineering team behind NZ's much-lauded Covid response, producing the Covid Tracer app. Using technology to take an active role in saving lives was one of his most defining achievements.

Carl builds products that solve real-world problems. His passion for both technology and film has driven him to found this startup. Underpinning it all is his immense desire to unlock the enormous creative potential in others.

With this technology, smart contracts can be used to provide more transparency, filmmakers can now incorporate royalty models directly, diminishing reliance on studio accounting practices that have been questionable. By offering audiences and investors part ownership in the produced intellectual property through smart contracts, filmmakers can grant exclusive access, elicit early feedback, and even grant voting rights through DAOs.
